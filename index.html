Choosing The Right Wedding Flowers For Your Big Day


When it comes to your wedding, you want everything to be perfect. And that includes the flowers. But with so many options available, how do you choose the right ones for your big day? Here are some tips to help you get started.

Read Post

Wedding Flowers: Consider your wedding theme


Your wedding flowers should complement your overall wedding theme. If you’re having a rustic-themed wedding, for example, then you’ll want to choose flowers that fit that style. The same is true for a more formal affair. Choose flowers that will help set the tone and mood of your wedding.

Another thing to consider when choosing your wedding flowers is the time of year you’re getting married. Different flowers are in bloom at different times, so you’ll want to make sure the blooms you choose will be available on your wedding day. If you’re getting married in the spring, for example, then you’ll want to choose spring flowers. The same is true for summer, fall, and winter weddings.

Wedding Flowers: Pick a color palette


After you’ve decided on a general theme, it’s time to start thinking about colors. Do you want something classic and elegant? Or are you going for something more colorful and fun? Once you’ve chosen a color palette, you can start narrowing down your flower options.

If you’re looking for classic elegance, white flowers are always a good choice. For a more colorful option, try incorporating some yellow flowers into your bouquet. Yellow is a cheerful color that is sure to add a touch of happiness to your special day.

Once you’ve decided on the overall look of your bouquet, it’s time to start thinking about the details. What type of flowers do you want? What colors do you want? How many flowers do you want?

Take some time to browse through different flower options before making your final decision. There are so many beautiful flowers out there, so don’t be afraid to experiment until you find the perfect combination for your wedding day.

Wedding Flowers: Work with a florist


A good florist will be able to help you choose the right flowers for your big day. They’ll also be able to work within your budget and find the best deals on the flowers you want.

When it comes to choosing a florist for your wedding, there are a few things you’ll want to keep in mind. First, you’ll want to find a florist who has experience with weddings. This will ensure that they’re familiar with the different types of flowers and arrangements that are typically used at weddings.


You’ll also want to find a florist who is willing to work within your budget. Wedding flowers can be expensive, so it’s important to find a florist who can give you the best value for your money.

Finally, you’ll want to make sure that the florist you choose is someone you feel comfortable working with. You should be able to communicate openly and easily with your florist so that you can get the results you’re looking for.

Consider the season


When you’re choosing your wedding date, keep in mind what time of year it is. That way, you can choose seasonal flowers that will be readily available (and less expensive). For example, roses are typically less expensive in the spring than in the summer.

If you’re set on a specific date but flexible on the time of year, try to book your venue at least 9 months in advance. That way, you’ll have a better chance of securing your dream location and getting vendors who are available on your preferred date.

Additionally, take into account how long you want to be married before you start planning. If you’re hoping for a shorter engagement, you’ll need to start sooner so that you don’t get overwhelmed closer to the big day.

Finally, remember that picking a date is just one part of the puzzle – ultimately, the most important thing is that you marry the love of your life on a day that works best for you as a couple!

Wedding Flowers: Don't forget the boutonnieres!


Don’t forget to pick out boutonnieres for the groom and groomsmen. You can either match them to the bride’s bouquet or choose a different color palette altogether. Keep in mind that the boutonnieres will be worn with the tuxedo, so choose something that complements the color and style of the suit.

When it comes to wedding flowers, there are a lot of options to choose from. But one thing you don’t want to forget is boutonnieres for the groom and groomsmen. These small floral accents can add a touch of elegance and class to the men’s attire, and they’re relatively easy to put together.

Don't wait until the last minute


Start shopping for your wedding flowers early so that you have plenty of time to find the perfect ones. Waiting until the last minute will only add unnecessary stress to your already busy life. Shop around at different stores and compare prices before making your final decision.

When it comes to wedding flowers, you want to make sure you choose the right ones. After all, they’ll be a big part of your big day! Here are a few tips to help you out:

  • Start shopping early. This will give you plenty of time to find the perfect flowers for your wedding. Waiting until the last minute will only add unnecessary stress to your already busy life.
  • Shop around. Compare prices at different stores before making your final decision.
  • Keep your budget in mind. Don’t overspend on your flowers just because you think they’re a necessary expense. There are plenty of beautiful, affordable options out there.
  • Work with a florist you trust. Find someone who you feel comfortable working with and who will understand your vision for your wedding flowers.

Compare prices


Make sure you compare prices before making a purchase. Wedding flowers can be expensive, so you’ll want to make sure you’re getting the best deal possible.

When it comes to your wedding, you want everything to be perfect. The flowers are no exception. But you don’t have to spend a fortune to get beautiful blooms for your big day.

There are ways to save on your wedding flowers without sacrificing quality or style. Here are a few tips:

  • Be flexible with your choices.

    • If you have your heart set on a certain type of flower, be open to other options that may be cheaper. For example, if you’re dead-set on roses, ask your florist about using carnations instead.

  • Go for seasonal flowers.

    • Flowers that are in season will be cheaper than those that aren’t. So, if you’re getting married in the spring, consider using daffodils or tulips in your arrangements.

  • Use less expensive filler flowers.

    • Filler flowers are typically used to add volume to an arrangement without adding a lot of costs. Baby’s breath is a popular filler flower because it’s cheap and goes with just about everything.

  • Consider potted plants.

    • Potted plants can be a cheaper alternative to cut flowers, and they make great centerpieces that your guests can take home with them at the end of the night.

  • Negotiate with your florist.

    • If you’re working with a professional florist, don’t be afraid to negotiate on price. If they know you’re working with a tight budget, they may be willing to give you a discount.

It’s important to compare prices when shopping for wedding flowers. But don’t sacrifice quality or style in the name of saving money. With a little bit of planning, you can have the beautiful blooms you’ve always dreamed of without breaking the bank.
